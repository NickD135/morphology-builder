Research Base

Word Labs is grounded in evidence-based literacy research. Here's a summary of the key principles and references.

🧠
Three Domains of Spelling Knowledge
Effective spelling instruction requires students to coordinate three interrelated forms of word knowledge. This framework is well established in structured literacy research and is reflected across the NSW English K–10 Syllabus.
Phonological
Awareness of sounds in spoken words — segmenting phonemes, counting syllables, manipulating onset and rime.
Orthographic
Knowledge of letter patterns and grapheme-phoneme correspondences — understanding that the same sound can be spelled multiple ways.
Morphological
Understanding meaningful word parts — prefixes, bases, suffixes, roots — and how they combine to build meaning and guide spelling.
💡
Key Research Principles
  • Explicit instruction matters. Structured, teacher-led instruction in morphology and phonics leads to significantly greater gains than incidental approaches (Bowers et al., 2010).
  • Morphological awareness transfers. Teaching morphemes improves not just spelling but also vocabulary and reading comprehension (Carlisle, 2010).
  • Poor spelling has cognitive costs. Difficulty encoding words slows writing and reduces compositional quality (Sumner et al., 2016).
  • Practice builds automaticity. Repeated, spaced retrieval of spelling patterns builds the automatic recall needed for fluent writing (Daffern, 2016).
  • Metalinguistic knowledge helps. Teaching students to talk about word structure (using terms like prefix, base, suffix) improves their ability to spell unfamiliar words (Daffern, 2018).
📑
Curriculum Alignment
  • NSW English K–10 Syllabus — Phonics and word knowledge strand: phonological, orthographic, and morphological components.
  • Australian Curriculum v9.0 — Language: Phonic and word knowledge sub-strand, Years 3–6 content descriptions.
  • NSW Literacy Continuum — Component A: Knowledge of Words (phonological, graphological, morphemic, etymological).
  • HPGE Policy — Extension Mode provides differentiated challenge for high-potential students.
📖
