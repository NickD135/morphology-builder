About Word Lab

A research-informed morphology and phonics platform built for Australian primary classrooms.

What is Word Lab?

Word Lab is a browser-based literacy platform designed for upper primary students (Years 3 to 6). It develops three core areas of word-level knowledge: morphological awareness (understanding how prefixes, bases, and suffixes combine to create meaning), phonemic awareness (hearing and manipulating individual sounds in words), and grapheme-phoneme correspondence (connecting written letter patterns to the sounds they represent).

All activities are structured and gamified. Students earn quarks (in-game currency), unlock scientist outfits, and build streaks while practising skills that directly support spelling, vocabulary, and reading comprehension. Teachers get a real-time dashboard showing progress, accuracy heatmaps, and intervention flags.

The Three-Tier Spelling Approach

English spelling draws from three major language layers. Word Lab's morpheme system maps directly onto this well-established framework, giving students the tools to decode and spell words from any tier.

Tier 1

Anglo-Saxon

Common, everyday words. Short bases with familiar prefixes and suffixes (e.g. un-help-ful, re-play-ing). These make up the majority of high-frequency English words.

Tier 2

Latin-Rooted

Academic vocabulary found across subject areas. Latin prefixes and roots combine predictably (e.g. con-struct-ion, trans-port). Critical for reading comprehension from Year 4 onward.

Tier 3

Greek-Rooted

Technical and discipline-specific words. Greek combining forms appear in science, maths, and geography (e.g. micro-phone, hyper-active, semi-circle).

By teaching students to recognise morphemes across all three tiers, Word Lab builds the transferable word knowledge that underpins strong spelling and vocabulary growth.

Research Base

Word Lab draws on a body of research demonstrating the importance of morphological awareness instruction for spelling and vocabulary development. Key influences include the work of Joanne Carlisle on morphological awareness and reading comprehension, Nagy and Anderson's research on the generative nature of morphological knowledge, and structured literacy principles that emphasise explicit, systematic teaching of word-level skills.

The platform aligns with the Australian Curriculum v9.0 English strand outcomes for language and literacy, particularly the sub-strands addressing phonics, word knowledge, and vocabulary. Activities are designed to support the gradual release model: students move from supported exploration (flashcards, morpheme builder) to independent application under time pressure (speed builder, breakdown blitz).

Word Lab was built by a practising NSW primary school teacher who saw a gap between evidence-based morphology instruction and the digital tools available in classrooms. Every activity is grounded in what works in real teaching, not abstract theory.

Component A of the NSW Spelling Continuum

The NSW Literacy Continuum identifies Component A: Knowledge of Words as a foundational element of spelling development. This component covers phonological knowledge (hearing sounds), graphological knowledge (letter-sound relationships), morphemic knowledge (meaningful word parts), and etymological knowledge (word origins).

Word Lab's activities directly support all four dimensions of Component A:

Phoneme Splitter and Sound Sorter develop phonological and graphological knowledge. Morpheme Builder, Breakdown Blitz, and Meaning Match-Up build morphemic knowledge. Root Lab supports etymological knowledge by exploring word origins across Anglo-Saxon, Latin, and Greek layers.

Teachers can use Word Lab alongside the NSW Literacy Continuum to identify where students sit on the spelling development trajectory and target activities accordingly. The teacher dashboard provides per-student accuracy data mapped to specific morphemes, sounds, and word patterns.
